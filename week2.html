Developing an Efficient and Sustainable Solar-Powered Desalination System: Addressing Global Freshwater Scarcity

Revised Problem Statement

Our project aims to develop an efficient and sustainable solar-powered desalination system that improves water desalination quality and output. The motivation behind this project is the increasing global freshwater scarcity and the potential of solar energy as a renewable solution.

To address this problem, we will focus on two key issues: enhancing solar evaporator efficiency and improving water desalination quality and output. These issues are crucial in ensuring the effectiveness and viability of our proposed solar-powered desalination system.

In the introduction/background section of our document, we will provide an overview of the evolution of desalination technologies, highlighting the challenges of conventional methods and the need for sustainable alternatives. We will also emphasize the significance of environmental sustainability in tackling global freshwater scarcity.

Additionally, we will discuss the advantages and limitations of solar energy for water desalination, underscoring its renewable nature and scalability. This discussion will showcase the relevance and potential impact of developing an efficient and sustainable solar-powered desalination system.

Overall, our Introduction/Background section will provide the necessary context and rationale for our project, outlining the evolution of desalination technologies, emphasizing environmental sustainability, and highlighting the potential of solar energy as a renewable resource in addressing global freshwater scarcity.
